Intro

Here I discuss the best way to combine the scores within a group. We want a method that we can do by hand to be able to perform a mixed model (and not a SEM) and also a method that provides a score in a unit that is convenient to interpret.

I use as an example the mean number of fixations at task 1 and task 2 for 24 month infants.

Arthmetic and gemotric mean

Given the distributions arithmetic mean or geometric mean shouldnt change much. Geometric mean is usually used to limit the effect of outliers.

  • Very correlated, would keep arithmetic as most common and understood and no problem with outliers

Z-scores

Z-scores are better in case of different variance across groups, doesnt seem to be the case here though.

  • colinear, no need to use z-scores

Random intercept

Will be equal to the arithmetic mean in the case of a model without covariates.
